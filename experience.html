Chassis Geometry & Carbon Fiber Mold
Ergonomics Composite Design Rule Compliance

The previous mold caused surface indentations and uneven resin curing. I redesigned the geometry to distribute pressure evenly and prevent warping during layup. Starting with 2D templates, I cross-checked each boundary (firewall, front hoop, steering, headrest) against the rulebook, built ergonomic rigs for driver fit testing, and calculated the car’s center of mass along both axes to optimize driver and component placement. Once validated, I expanded the model to 3D, coordinating with other subteams to ensure structural integration, elbow clearance, and visibility.

CFD Workflow Automation
Bash / sbatch Aerodynamics Process Optimization

To speed up aerodynamic testing, I automated the CFD workflow using shell scripts that generate sbatch templates automatically. Users can input geometry and key parameters once, and the system builds, runs, and extracts results automatically — drag, downforce, and coefficient data are compiled for quick comparison between design iterations. This reduced setup time and simplified batch testing for the aero team.

Strain Gauge & Sensor System
PCB / Altium Signal Stability Vibration Control

Designed and prototyped PCBs for strain gauge sensors mounted throughout the chassis. Low-cost gauges can lose accuracy under vibration, so we explored reinforcement methods such as epoxy bonding, silicone potting, and vibration-dampening mounts to improve reliability. I’m also developing a modular data hub (“main brain”) to collect readings from multiple sensors for real-time load monitoring and performance analysis.
